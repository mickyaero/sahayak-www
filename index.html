§ I — Thesis

"Mess closes at 9.
Students stay up till 2.
The five-hour gap
is where Sahayak lives."

In India, the campus dining infrastructure stops at nine in the evening. Quick-commerce halts at the hostel gate. Sahayak fills the window humans don't — autonomously, indifferent to weather, awake when the city sleeps.

Every Sahayak run is a parametric curve,
weighted by hunger, weather, and time.
